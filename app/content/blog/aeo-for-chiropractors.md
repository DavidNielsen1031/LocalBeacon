# AEO for Chiropractors: How to Get Your Practice Recommended by AI

When someone's lower back is screaming at 10 PM and they type "best chiropractor near me for back pain" into ChatGPT, what happens next matters enormously for your practice. Either your name comes up — or a competitor's does.

That's the core of **Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)**: making your chiropractic practice the one AI systems confidently recommend when patients are searching for care. Unlike traditional SEO, which focuses on ranking in a list of blue links, AEO focuses on becoming the *answer* — the practice an AI cites by name, with context, in a conversational response.

Here's how to do it.

---

## Why Chiropractic Is Perfectly Positioned for AEO

Chiropractic care has a structural advantage when it comes to AI search: patients ask *highly specific questions*. "What's the difference between a spinal adjustment and spinal decompression?" "Is chiropractic safe for sciatica?" "How many sessions does it take to fix a bulging disc?"

These are exactly the kinds of queries that AI systems want to answer well — and they pull from practices whose websites *clearly explain* what they do, who they treat, and what results to expect.

A chiropractor who publishes authoritative, specific, patient-friendly content will consistently outperform competitors who have a generic "services" page and nothing else.

---

## Step 1: Build Condition-Specific Service Pages (Not Just "Chiropractic Care")

The single biggest mistake chiropractors make online is having one generic page that says "We treat back pain, neck pain, and headaches." AI systems can't recommend you for specific conditions if you haven't clearly documented them.

**Instead, build individual pages for each condition you treat:**

- Lower back pain / lumbar disc herniation
- Neck pain / cervical disc issues
- Sciatica / piriformis syndrome
- Headaches and migraines
- Sports injuries (with specific sports if relevant — runner's knee, tennis elbow)
- Auto accident / whiplash recovery
- Pediatric chiropractic (if offered)
- Prenatal chiropractic (if offered)
- Workplace ergonomics / posture correction

Each page should answer these questions at minimum:
1. What is this condition, and what causes it?
2. How does chiropractic care treat it?
3. How many visits does treatment typically require?
4. What should a patient expect during their first adjustment?
5. What results can they realistically expect?

Pages structured this way get pulled into AI answers because they *directly answer what patients are asking*.

---

## Step 2: Optimize Your Google Business Profile for AI Queries

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the foundation of your local AI visibility. AI systems use GBP data as a primary trust signal for local recommendations.

**Chiropractic-specific GBP optimizations:**

**Choose the right primary category.** "Chiropractor" is your primary. Add secondary categories like "Sports medicine clinic," "Massage therapist" (if you offer it), or "Physical therapist" only if genuinely relevant — don't over-tag.

**List every service explicitly.** Use GBP's Services section to list each condition you treat and technique you use (diversified technique, Gonstead, activator, dry needling, soft tissue work, etc.). Each service entry is a signal to AI about your scope of practice.

**Use the Q&A section proactively.** Don't wait for patients to ask questions. Seed the Q&A yourself with the questions new patients always ask:
- "Do you accept [insurance name]?"
- "Do you treat sciatica?"
- "Do you offer same-day appointments?"
- "Is chiropractic safe during pregnancy?"

**Post weekly with educational content.** GBP Posts that explain conditions, share patient education tips, or announce technique training you've completed signal to AI that your practice is active and authoritative.

## Step 3: Build a Review Strategy Around Specificity

For chiropractors, reviews aren't just social proof — they're data that AI systems parse to understand what you're actually good at.

A review that says "Great chiropractor, highly recommend!" tells AI almost nothing about your specialty. A review that says "I came in with severe sciatica after three months of pain that wasn't responding to anything. Dr. Chen adjusted my L4-L5 and prescribed specific stretches. Within six weeks I was back to running" — that's a review AI can use to recommend your practice for sciatica patients.

**Practical steps:**

- After a patient reaches a milestone (pain resolved, back to sport, cleared for discharge), send a follow-up text: "We're so glad you're feeling better! Would you mind sharing your experience in a Google review? It helps other patients in similar situations find us."
- Give them a direct link to your GBP review page — remove every friction point.
- When requesting reviews, gently frame it: "Other patients looking for help with [their condition] will benefit from knowing about your experience."

You don't write their reviews. But you can remind them what you treated and invite them to share the outcome.

**Target at minimum:** 50+ reviews with an average of 4.5 stars before expecting meaningful AI visibility. The practices AI recommends most often have 100+ reviews with specific condition mentions.

---

## Step 4: Get Listed in Chiropractic-Specific Directories

AI systems weight citations from authoritative, industry-specific directories heavily. For chiropractors, these directories provide the trust signals that separate you from a generic business listing.

**Priority directories for chiropractors:**

- **Psychology Today's chiropractor finder** (yes — patients often start here)
- **ChiroDirectory.com**
- **American Chiropractic Association member directory** (if you're a member)
- **Healthgrades** — a major trust signal for any healthcare provider
- **Zocdoc** — patients book directly here; AI knows this
- **WebMD's doctor finder**
- **US News Health** provider listings
- **Vitals.com**
- **RateMDs**

Beyond chiropractic-specific directories, make sure your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) is consistent across Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, and the major data aggregators (Neustar Localeze, Data Axle, Foursquare).

**Inconsistent NAP is one of the top reasons AI systems lose confidence in a local business listing** and recommend a competitor instead.

---

## Step 5: Add Schema Markup for Healthcare Providers

Schema markup is structured data that you add to your website to help AI systems and search engines understand exactly what your business is and does. For chiropractors, two schema types are critical:

**`LocalBusiness` schema** with `@type: "Chiropractor"` (this is a recognized schema type in the MedicalBusiness hierarchy) tells AI precisely what category of practice you are.

**`MedicalSpecialty` and `medicalSpecialty` property** allow you to declare the conditions and specialties you treat in a machine-readable format.

**`FAQPage` schema** on your condition pages wraps your Q&A content in a format that AI systems are designed to consume and cite.

If you're on WordPress, plugins like Yoast SEO or RankMath can help you add these without touching code. On custom sites, ask your developer to implement JSON-LD structured data.

Even basic `LocalBusiness` schema with correct hours, address, phone, and `@type: "Chiropractor"` puts you ahead of most competitors who have no schema at all.

## The Compound Effect: Why This Works

Each of these five steps reinforces the others. Condition-specific pages give AI content to cite. A complete GBP tells AI your practice is legitimate and active. Detailed reviews confirm what you actually treat. Directory citations prove you're a recognized healthcare provider. Schema markup makes all of it machine-readable.

A practice that does all five doesn't just show up in AI answers — it shows up *confidently*, with specific details, in a way that makes patients want to call.

The chiropractors who invest in AEO now are building an asset that compounds over time. As AI search continues to grow as a primary way patients find healthcare providers, the gap between AI-visible practices and invisible ones will only widen.

**Start with your condition pages.** That single step — turning "back pain" into five specific, well-written pages — will do more for your AI visibility than almost anything else.
