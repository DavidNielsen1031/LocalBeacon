When a homeowner asks ChatGPT "who's the best HVAC company near me," or a patient asks Google's AI Overview "find a dentist that takes my insurance," the AI isn't browsing Yelp and guessing. It's pulling from a specific, structured set of signals — and most local businesses haven't optimized for any of them.

That's a problem. And it's also an opportunity.

AI-powered search is growing fast. A 2025 study found that over 40% of consumers have used an AI tool to research a local service provider. That number will only climb. The businesses that figure this out now will be the ones AI recommends six months from now.

Here are five concrete things you can do — regardless of your industry — to show up when customers are asking AI for recommendations.

---

## 1. Build Service Pages That Answer Real Questions

The #1 mistake local businesses make is having a "Services" page that just lists what they offer. That's not how AI search works.

AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity are trained to answer specific questions. When someone asks "what's included in a furnace tune-up" or "how long does a root canal take," they want an answer — not a sales pitch. If your website answers those questions clearly, you become a source the AI trusts.

**What to do:**
- Create individual pages for each core service (not one big catch-all page)
- Include a short FAQ section on each service page (3–5 questions customers actually ask)
- Answer the questions directly in plain language — don't bury the answer after paragraphs of fluff
- Include pricing ranges when you can (AI tools love transparency here)

A page titled "Water Heater Replacement in [City]" that explains cost ranges, how long it takes, and what to expect will outperform a generic "Plumbing Services" page every time.

---

## 2. Treat Your Google Business Profile Like a Second Website

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is one of the most powerful signals feeding AI Overviews in Google Search. It's also the most neglected.

Most business owners set it up once and forget it. AI systems, meanwhile, are reading it constantly — checking whether your hours are accurate, whether you've posted recently, whether your business description mentions the right services.

**What to do:**
- Write a business description that actually describes what you do and who you serve (don't waste it on generic marketing language)
- Use the "Services" section to list every service with a short description — this is often directly pulled into AI responses
- Post at least once a week (photos, offers, or brief tips work well)
- Keep your hours updated, including holiday hours
- Add your service area cities explicitly — AI uses this for "near me" queries

If your profile hasn't been touched in six months, block 30 minutes this week and update it. The improvement is usually immediate.

---

## 3. Generate Reviews That Mention Specific Services and Locations

Reviews are trust signals — not just for human readers, but for AI systems. But there's a nuance most businesses miss: **generic reviews help less than specific ones**.

A review that says "Great company, highly recommend!" is fine. A review that says "Called ABC Plumbing on a Sunday when our water heater burst. They were at my house in Lakewood within two hours and had it replaced by 5 PM. Fair price, no upselling" is an AI goldmine. It contains a service (water heater replacement), a location (Lakewood), a timeline, and a trust signal (fair pricing, no upselling).

**What to do:**
- After completing a job, send a follow-up text or email asking for a review
- Coach customers with a brief prompt: "If you have a moment, a review mentioning the service we did and where you're located helps a lot"
- Respond to every review (more on this below)
- Aim for a steady flow of 2–4 new reviews per month — consistency matters more than volume spikes

The combination of service-specific language and location mentions in reviews helps AI systems confidently recommend you when someone nearby asks about that exact service.

---

## 4. Respond to Every Review — Including the Negative Ones

This one surprises a lot of business owners. Review *responses* are not just about customer relations. They're content that AI systems read.

When you respond to a review and mention your business name, location, and the service performed, you're reinforcing those signals in a format AI can process. And when you respond well to negative reviews, you're demonstrating professionalism — which AI tools increasingly factor into trust assessments.

**What to do:**
- Respond to every new review within 48 hours
- In positive review responses, naturally include your business name, city, and service: "Thanks so much — we're always happy to help with roof repairs in the Denver area!"
- For negative reviews, acknowledge the issue, apologize sincerely, and invite them to contact you directly (don't argue in public)
- Keep responses brief but genuine — a few sentences is enough

A business with 80 reviews and zero responses looks neglected to AI systems. A business with 80 reviews and consistent, professional responses looks trustworthy.

---

## 5. Get Listed in the Right Directories — and Keep Them Consistent

AI tools cross-reference information about your business across multiple sources. If your name, address, and phone number (NAP) are inconsistent across different directories, it creates confusion — and AI systems resolve that confusion by recommending someone else.

But directories aren't just about NAP consistency. The *right* directories also pass trust signals that carry weight with AI tools.

**What to do:**
- Audit your listings on Google, Yelp, Facebook, Bing Places, and Apple Maps — make sure NAP is identical across all of them
- Get listed in industry-specific directories relevant to your trade (Angi, HomeAdvisor, Houzz for home services; Healthgrades and Zocdoc for healthcare; Avvo for legal; etc.)
- Add your business to local Chamber of Commerce directories and city business directories when available
- Check for duplicate listings and merge or remove them

The goal is a consistent, coherent digital footprint that lets AI systems confidently identify who you are, where you are, and what you do.

---

## Start With One, Build From There

If this list feels overwhelming, start with your Google Business Profile. It takes under an hour to optimize properly, and the impact is visible within weeks.

Then work your way through the list. Each step compounds — better service pages feed into better reviews, which feed into better AI recommendations, which bring in more customers, which generate more reviews.

This is the flywheel that local businesses with strong AI visibility are already spinning. The good news: most of your competitors haven't started yet.
