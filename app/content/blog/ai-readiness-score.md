You ran the free AI readiness check on your business — and now you're looking at a number. Maybe it's a 35. Maybe it's a 72. But what does it actually mean, and what should you do about it?

Let's walk through it.

## What Is the AI Readiness Score?

The AI Readiness Score is a 0-100 assessment of how well-prepared your business is to be found and recommended by AI search engines — tools like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Claude, and Siri.

It's not a measure of your overall business quality. A fantastic plumber with no online presence might score a 15. A mediocre one with a well-optimized Google profile and structured website content might score a 75. The score reflects your **digital visibility to AI**, not your skill at your trade.

## How the Score Is Calculated

The AI Readiness Score evaluates your business across several key areas. Here's what gets checked:

### Google Business Profile (25 points)

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the single most important factor in both local SEO and AI discoverability. The score evaluates:

- **Profile completeness** — Are your hours, services, description, categories, and service areas filled in? Missing fields mean AI has less information to work with.
- **Post recency** — When did you last post to your GBP? Businesses that post weekly score higher than those that haven't posted in months.
- **Review activity** — How many reviews do you have? What's your average rating? Have you responded to recent reviews?
- **Photos** — Profiles with recent photos get more engagement and signal an active business.

**How to improve:** The fastest win is completing every field in your GBP and starting weekly posts. Even simple "What's new" updates help.

### Website Content (20 points)

AI engines crawl your website to understand what you do and where you do it. The score checks:

- **Service pages** — Do you have dedicated pages for each service you offer? A single "Services" page with a bulleted list isn't enough.
- **Location pages** — If you serve multiple cities, do you have pages targeting each one? AI uses these to match you with location-specific queries.
- **About page** — Does your website clearly explain who you are, how long you've been in business, and what makes you different?
- **Contact information** — Is your NAP (name, address, phone) prominently displayed and consistent with your GBP?

**How to improve:** Start with service pages. If you offer 8 services, create 8 pages. Each page should include what the service involves, who it's for, your service area, and pricing information if applicable.

### FAQ Content (15 points)

FAQ pages are gold for AI visibility. AI models are literally trained to match questions with answers, so well-structured FAQs give you a direct advantage:

- **FAQ presence** — Does your website have FAQ content?
- **Depth** — Are there at least 10 substantive Q&A pairs?
- **Schema markup** — Are your FAQs marked up with FAQPage schema so AI can parse them programmatically?
- **Relevance** — Do the FAQs answer real customer questions, not just generic filler?

**How to improve:** Write down the 15 questions your customers ask most often. Answer each one in 2-4 sentences. Add FAQ schema markup to the page. This single step can move your score significantly.

### Structured Data (15 points)

Structured data (schema markup) is machine-readable code that tells AI exactly what your business information means:

- **LocalBusiness schema** — Basic business information in a format AI can parse directly
- **Service schema** — Structured information about each service you offer
- **FAQ schema** — Question-and-answer pairs marked up for AI consumption
- **Review/AggregateRating schema** — Your review data in structured format

**How to improve:** If you're not technical, this is one area where a tool really helps. LocalBeacon generates schema markup automatically. If you're doing it yourself, start with LocalBusiness schema — it's the most impactful.

### AI Discovery File (10 points)

Your llms.txt file is a dedicated resource for AI crawlers:

- **Presence** — Does your website have an llms.txt file at the root domain?
- **Completeness** — Does it include services, locations, contact info, and differentiators?
- **Formatting** — Is it properly structured using the llms.txt convention?

**How to improve:** Create an llms.txt file. It takes 10-15 minutes and can immediately improve your AI discoverability. See our [guide to llms.txt](/blog/llms-txt-explained) for a step-by-step walkthrough.

### Review Profile (15 points)

Reviews are a trust signal for both search engines and AI:

- **Volume** — More reviews indicate a more established business
- **Rating** — Higher average ratings lead to more favorable recommendations
- **Recency** — Recent reviews are weighted more heavily than old ones
- **Response rate** — Responding to reviews (especially negative ones) signals professionalism

**How to improve:** Ask satisfied customers to leave a Google review. Respond to every review — positive and negative — within 24-48 hours. A simple, professional response shows you're attentive and engaged.

## Score Ranges: What They Mean

### 0-25: Not Visible to AI
AI assistants almost certainly won't recommend your business. You likely have a minimal or outdated Google Business Profile, few reviews, and limited website content. The good news: there's a lot of low-hanging fruit.

### 26-50: Starting to Show Up
You have some basics in place — probably a GBP and a website — but you're missing key elements that AI engines look for. Adding FAQ content, schema markup, and an llms.txt file can push you into the next tier.

### 51-75: Competitive
AI may already be recommending your business for some queries. You have a solid foundation, and targeted improvements to your weakest areas will make a noticeable difference.

### 76-100: AI-Optimized
Your business is well-positioned for AI search. You're ahead of most competitors. Focus on maintaining freshness (weekly posts, regular review responses) and monitoring AI recommendations to stay ahead.

## The Most Impactful Improvements (Ranked)

If you want to improve your score as quickly as possible, prioritize these:

1. **Complete your Google Business Profile** — biggest single impact
2. **Start posting to GBP weekly** — signals freshness and activity
3. **Create FAQ content with schema markup** — direct AI value
4. **Respond to all reviews** — trust signal plus engagement
5. **Create an llms.txt file** — quick win, most competitors don't have one
6. **Build service-specific pages** — helps AI match you to specific queries
7. **Add LocalBusiness schema markup** — machine-readable business data
8. **Create location pages** — captures multi-city queries

## How Often Should You Check Your Score?

We recommend checking monthly. Your score can change based on:

- New or expired Google posts
- New reviews (or lack of responses to existing ones)
- Website content changes
- Competitor activity (which indirectly affects AI recommendations)

Monthly check-ins help you spot trends and catch issues early — like a review that needs a response or a GBP post schedule that's slipped.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the AI Readiness Score the same as an SEO score?**
No. SEO scores focus on Google Search ranking factors. The AI Readiness Score specifically measures your visibility to AI answer engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity. There's overlap (both care about GBP and content quality), but they're measuring different things.

**My competitor has a lower rating but a higher score. Why?**
The score isn't just about review rating — it factors in response rate, content freshness, structured data, FAQ quality, and more. A business with a 4.5 rating but great content, weekly posts, and schema markup will outscore a 4.8-rated business with a bare-bones online presence.

**Can I game the AI Readiness Score?**
Not in any meaningful way. The score reflects genuine signals that AI engines use: content quality, data structure, freshness, and review engagement. Improving your score means improving your actual AI visibility.

**Does a high score guarantee AI recommendations?**
No. The score indicates readiness — how well-positioned you are. AI recommendations also depend on the specific query, location, competition, and the AI model's training data. But higher scores strongly correlate with more frequent recommendations.

**How does LocalBeacon calculate this score?**
The score is based on a 15-point audit across the categories described above. Each factor is weighted by its impact on AI discoverability, based on research into how major AI models source and prioritize local business information.
