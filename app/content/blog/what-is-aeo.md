When someone asks ChatGPT "who's the best plumber near me?" — does your business show up?

If you're not sure, you're not alone. Most local businesses have spent years optimizing for Google Search. But the way people find businesses is changing fast. AI assistants — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Siri, Alexa — are answering more and more of these questions directly. And they don't use Google's algorithm to pick who to recommend.

This is where **Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)** comes in.

## What Is AEO?

AEO is the practice of optimizing your business's online presence so that AI-powered answer engines can find, understand, and recommend you.

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking in a list of 10 blue links. AEO focuses on being the **answer** — the business that AI chooses to mention when a customer asks a question.

Think of it this way:

- **SEO question:** "How do I rank higher on Google?"
- **AEO question:** "How do I get ChatGPT to recommend my business?"

Both matter. But AEO is growing faster than most business owners realize.

## Why AEO Matters for Local Businesses

Here's what's happening right now:

**AI search is exploding.** ChatGPT has over 200 million weekly users. Google AI Overviews appear in almost half of all searches. Perplexity is growing 40% month over month. These aren't fringe tools — they're becoming the default way people search.

**AI assistants give direct recommendations.** When someone searches Google, they see a list and choose. When someone asks an AI assistant, they get a specific answer: "Based on reviews and services offered, I'd recommend ABC Plumbing." If you're not the recommendation, you're invisible.

**Local searches are shifting to AI.** "Find me a dentist who's open Saturday and takes Delta Dental" — that's a question people are already asking AI assistants. The AI pulls from structured data, reviews, business profiles, and website content to generate its answer.

## How AI Assistants Decide Who to Recommend

AI answer engines pull information from several sources:

1. **Your Google Business Profile** — hours, services, reviews, photos, posts
2. **Your website content** — service pages, FAQ sections, location pages
3. **Structured data (schema markup)** — machine-readable information about your business
4. **Third-party citations** — directory listings, review sites, industry databases
5. **llms.txt files** — a new standard that tells AI crawlers exactly what your business does

The businesses that provide clear, structured, and comprehensive information across all these channels are the ones that get recommended.

## What AEO Looks Like in Practice

Here's what a local business optimized for AI answer engines typically has:

### Structured FAQ Pages
Instead of a single FAQ page with five generic questions, AEO-optimized businesses have detailed FAQ content organized by topic. Each question-and-answer pair is marked up with FAQ schema so AI engines can parse them directly.

### Service-Specific Pages
A page titled "Plumbing Services" is too vague for AI. Pages like "Water Heater Installation in Burnsville, MN" give AI engines the specific, localized content they need to match your business to detailed queries.

### An llms.txt File
This is a new convention — a plain text file at your website's root that tells AI crawlers about your business in a format they're designed to read. Think of it like robots.txt, but for AI. It includes your business name, services, locations, and what makes you different.

### Fresh Google Business Profile Content
AI engines heavily weight recency. Businesses that post weekly updates to their Google Business Profile signal that they're active and reliable. Stale profiles get deprioritized.

### Complete Schema Markup
LocalBusiness schema, Service schema, FAQ schema, Review schema — these structured data types help AI understand exactly what your business offers, where you operate, and what customers think of you.

## How to Get Started with AEO

You don't need to become a technical SEO expert. Here's a practical starting point:

**1. Audit your current AI visibility.** Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI about your services in your area. See if your business comes up. If it doesn't, that's your baseline.

**2. Update your Google Business Profile.** Make sure your hours, services, service areas, and business description are complete and current. Start posting weekly updates.

**3. Add FAQ content to your website.** Write out the 10-15 questions your customers actually ask you. Answer them thoroughly. Add FAQ schema markup.

**4. Create location-specific pages.** If you serve multiple cities, create a page for each one with specific local content.

**5. Generate an llms.txt file.** This is a quick win that most competitors haven't done yet. It's a plain text file that takes minutes to create but can immediately improve your AI discoverability.

**6. Monitor and iterate.** AEO isn't a one-time project. As AI engines evolve, the signals they use will change. Monthly check-ins help you stay ahead.

## AEO Is Not Replacing SEO

Let's be clear: SEO still matters. Google Search still drives the majority of local business discovery. AEO is an additional layer — it's about making sure you're visible in the new channels where customers are looking, not abandoning the channels that already work.

Think of it as diversifying your marketing. Five years ago, you probably added social media to your marketing mix without dropping your website. AEO is the same idea: a new channel that works alongside everything else.

## The Bottom Line

AI assistants are already recommending local businesses to real customers. The businesses that get recommended are the ones with clear, structured, and up-to-date online presences.

AEO isn't complicated. It's just about making it easy for AI to understand what you do, where you do it, and why customers should choose you. Start with the basics — a complete Google profile, FAQ content, and an llms.txt file — and build from there.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is AEO only for tech companies?**
Not at all. AEO is especially valuable for local service businesses — plumbers, dentists, HVAC companies, roofers, landscapers. These are the exact queries people are asking AI assistants about.

**Do I need to hire an AEO expert?**
You can get started on your own with the steps above. Tools like LocalBeacon automate most of the heavy lifting — generating FAQ pages, creating llms.txt files, posting to Google, and adding schema markup.

**How long does AEO take to show results?**
Some changes (like adding an llms.txt file or updating your Google profile) can show results within days. Others (like building location pages) may take a few weeks to be indexed by AI crawlers.

**Will AEO replace SEO eventually?**
It's unlikely in the near term. SEO and AEO will coexist. But the share of traffic coming from AI-driven sources is growing every month, so businesses that start AEO now will have a significant advantage.

**How do I check if AI assistants recommend my business?**
Try asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI questions like "Who's the best [your service] in [your city]?" If your business doesn't appear, you have room to improve.
