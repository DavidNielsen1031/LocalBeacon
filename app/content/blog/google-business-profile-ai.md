Your Google Business Profile (GBP) isn't just for Google Maps anymore.

When ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, or Siri recommends a local business, your GBP is one of the primary data sources they pull from. The information in your profile — your services, hours, reviews, posts, and photos — directly shapes whether AI assistants recommend you or your competitor.

Most business owners think of GBP as a "set it and forget it" listing. That was fine when Google Search was the only game in town. In 2026, your GBP is the foundation of your AI visibility.

## Why AI Assistants Rely on Google Business Profile

AI assistants need reliable, structured data about local businesses. When someone asks "Who's a good plumber in Eagan?", the AI has to quickly assess which businesses are legitimate, active, and well-reviewed.

Google Business Profile is the best source for this because:

**It's verified.** Google verifies business ownership, which means the data is more trustworthy than random web pages. AI models assign higher confidence to verified sources.

**It's structured.** GBP data comes in a consistent format — name, address, phone, hours, services, reviews. AI models can parse this data instantly without having to scrape and interpret messy web pages.

**It's comprehensive.** A well-maintained GBP includes business description, service list, service areas, hours, photos, posts, reviews, and Q&A. That's a rich dataset for AI to work with.

**It's updated.** GBP reflects real-time changes — updated hours, new photos, recent posts, fresh reviews. AI models weight recency heavily, and GBP provides a clear timeline of business activity.

## The GBP Fields That Matter Most for AI

Not all GBP fields are equally important for AI visibility. Here's what matters most:

### Business Description
This is your pitch. AI reads this to understand what your business does. Be specific and complete:

**Weak:** "We provide quality service to our community."
**Strong:** "Johnson Plumbing provides emergency plumbing repair, water heater installation, drain cleaning, and bathroom remodeling for homes and businesses in the south Minneapolis metro. Licensed, bonded, and insured with 15 years of experience."

The strong version gives AI everything it needs: services, location, credentials, and experience. The weak version gives nothing.

### Services List
GBP lets you add specific services under your business categories. Every service you list is a potential query match. If someone asks AI "Who does water softener installation near me?" and you've listed that service, you're more likely to be recommended.

**Action:** List every service you offer. Be specific. "Drain cleaning" is better than "plumbing services." Include variations: "tankless water heater installation," "water heater repair," "water heater replacement."

### Service Areas
If you serve multiple cities, list every one. AI assistants are heavily location-aware. When someone asks about services "in Lakeville," the AI cross-references your service areas. If Lakeville isn't listed, you might be skipped even if you're 10 minutes away.

**Action:** Add every city, suburb, and neighborhood you serve. Most GBP listings have far too few service areas listed.

### Business Categories
Your primary and secondary categories tell AI what type of business you are. Choose the most specific categories available.

**Action:** Set your primary category to your core service. Add secondary categories for each service line. A roofing company might use "Roofing Contractor" as primary, with "Gutter Installation Service" and "Siding Contractor" as secondary.

### Hours of Operation
AI assistants often factor in availability. When someone asks "Who can fix my furnace today?" at 6 PM, the AI might prioritize businesses with extended or emergency hours.

**Action:** Keep your hours accurate. If you offer emergency service, make sure that's reflected. Update seasonal hours promptly.

## Google Posts: The Freshness Signal

Google Business Profile posts are one of the most underused features — and one of the most impactful for AI visibility.

### Why Posts Matter for AI

AI models treat content freshness as a quality signal. A business that posted last week looks active and engaged. A business that hasn't posted in 6 months looks potentially closed or disengaged.

When an AI assistant chooses between two similar businesses — same ratings, same services, same area — the one with recent activity often gets the nod.

### What to Post

You don't need to write novels. Here's what works:

- **Service highlights:** "This week we completed a tankless water heater installation in Apple Valley. Tankless units save up to 30% on energy costs compared to traditional tanks."
- **Seasonal tips:** "Minnesota winters are hard on pipes. Here are 3 signs your pipes might be at risk of freezing..."
- **Before/after updates:** "Just finished a bathroom remodel in Burnsville. Swipe to see the before and after."
- **Promotions:** "Booking spring AC tune-ups now. Schedule before April 15 and save $25."

### How Often to Post

Weekly is the sweet spot. It maintains the freshness signal without being overwhelming. Daily posting doesn't provide much additional benefit for AI visibility, and monthly posting lets the freshness signal fade.

## Reviews: The Trust Layer

Reviews are a trust multiplier for AI recommendations. Here's how AI uses them:

### Volume
More reviews = more data for AI to assess. A business with 200 reviews gives AI a clearer picture than one with 8 reviews.

### Rating
Higher ratings lead to more favorable recommendations, but it's not just about having a 5.0. AI models are sophisticated enough to be suspicious of perfect ratings with few reviews. A 4.7 with 300 reviews often beats a 5.0 with 15 reviews.

### Recency
Reviews from this month matter more than reviews from three years ago. Recent reviews indicate ongoing quality. If your last review was in 2024, AI might wonder if you're still active.

### Response Rate
This is the one most businesses miss. Responding to reviews — especially negative ones — signals professionalism and customer care. AI models notice when a business engages with its reviews versus ignoring them.

A professional response to a 2-star review ("We're sorry to hear about your experience. We'd like to make it right — please call us at...") tells AI that this business handles problems well.

### What Reviewers Say
AI doesn't just look at the star rating — it reads the review text. Reviews that mention specific services ("Great job fixing our garbage disposal"), locations ("Came out to our house in Lakeville right away"), and qualities ("On time, professional, cleaned up after") give AI rich data to work with.

**How to leverage this:** When asking for reviews, gently suggest that customers mention the service, location, or experience. "If you have a minute, a Google review mentioning the water heater install would really help us out."

## Photos: More Than Aesthetics

Photos serve two purposes for AI:

1. **Activity signal:** Recent photos indicate an active business. AI treats photo uploads similarly to posts — they're a freshness indicator.
2. **Verification:** Photos of your team, vehicles, completed work, and storefront help AI confirm that your business is real and operational.

You don't need professional photography. Phone photos of completed work, your team, or your service vehicles are perfectly effective.

## Q&A Section: Hidden AI Value

GBP has a Q&A section that many businesses ignore. This is a missed opportunity:

- Questions asked here are often the same questions AI gets asked
- Your answers become part of your GBP's content footprint
- Well-answered Q&A helps AI understand your business better

**Action:** Proactively add and answer common questions in your GBP's Q&A section. This is essentially free FAQ content that feeds directly into your AI visibility.

## A GBP Optimization Checklist for AI

Here's a priority-ordered checklist:

1. Complete every field in your profile — no blanks
2. Write a detailed, specific business description (750 characters max)
3. List every service you offer, using specific terms
4. Add every city and neighborhood you serve
5. Set accurate hours, including special/emergency hours
6. Post at least weekly (service highlights, tips, or updates)
7. Respond to every review within 48 hours
8. Upload photos monthly (completed work, team, seasonal)
9. Add Q&A entries for your most common customer questions
10. Choose the most specific business categories available

If you do all 10, your Google Business Profile will be one of the strongest AI data sources in your local market.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know if AI assistants are using my GBP data?**
Try asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI about your services in your area. If they mention your business name, hours, or services, they're pulling from your GBP (directly or indirectly).

**I claimed my GBP years ago but never updated it. Where do I start?**
Start with your business description and services list — these have the biggest impact. Then move to service areas and hours. Once the basics are solid, start weekly posts.

**How long does it take for GBP changes to affect AI recommendations?**
GBP changes are typically reflected within days to weeks. Posts and reviews are picked up quickly. Structural changes (new services, updated description) may take longer to influence AI models.

**Should I pay for GBP features like promoted posts?**
Paid GBP features primarily boost visibility in Google Maps, not AI assistants. For AI visibility specifically, free features (posts, reviews, Q&A, complete profile) are what matter.

**Can I manage multiple GBP listings?**
Yes. If you have multiple locations, each should have its own fully optimized GBP listing. Tools like LocalBeacon can manage multiple listings from a single dashboard.
