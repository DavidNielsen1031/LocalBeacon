You've probably heard of robots.txt — the file that tells Google's crawlers what to index on your website. Now there's a new file doing something similar for AI assistants: **llms.txt**.

It's simple, it's free to create, and most of your competitors don't have one yet.

## What Is llms.txt?

llms.txt is a plain text file that lives at the root of your website (e.g., `yourwebsite.com/llms.txt`). It's designed specifically for large language models (LLMs) — the AI behind tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google's AI features.

The file gives AI a structured, easy-to-read summary of what your business does, where you operate, what services you offer, and what makes you different.

Think of it as your business's elevator pitch — but written for AI instead of a person.

## Why Does llms.txt Matter?

When an AI assistant tries to answer a question like "Who's a good electrician in Minneapolis?", it pulls information from many sources: your website, your Google Business Profile, review sites, directories, and more.

The problem is that your website might have dozens of pages, and AI has to figure out which information is relevant and accurate. Sometimes it gets it wrong. Sometimes it skips you entirely because it couldn't parse your site efficiently.

llms.txt solves this by giving AI a single, authoritative source of truth about your business. It says: "Here's exactly who we are, what we do, and where we do it — in a format you can read instantly."

### The Competitive Advantage

Right now, very few local businesses have an llms.txt file. This is a genuine first-mover advantage. As AI search grows (and it's growing fast), the businesses that make it easy for AI to understand them will be the ones that get recommended.

It's like having a great Google Business Profile in 2015 — it wasn't required, but the businesses that had one got found more often.

## What Goes in an llms.txt File?

A typical llms.txt file for a local business includes:

### Business Identity
- Business name
- What you do (one clear sentence)
- Location and service areas
- Years in business
- Licenses and certifications

### Services
- Complete list of services you offer
- Specialties or areas of focus
- Emergency or after-hours availability

### Contact Information
- Phone number
- Email
- Website URL
- Physical address

### Differentiators
- What makes you different from competitors
- Awards or recognition
- Customer satisfaction stats
- Unique offerings

### Customer Information
- Who your typical customer is
- Common problems you solve
- Service area boundaries

## Best Practices for llms.txt

**Be specific.** Don't say "We offer many services." List every service. AI needs specifics to match you to specific queries.

**Include your service areas.** List every city, neighborhood, and zip code you serve. AI assistants heavily weight location when answering local queries.

**Keep it updated.** When you add a new service, expand to a new area, or change your hours, update llms.txt. Stale information can lead to bad recommendations.

**Use plain language.** Write like you're explaining your business to a new customer. Avoid jargon, abbreviations, and internal terminology.

**Don't stuff keywords.** AI models are sophisticated. Writing naturally about what you do is more effective than repeating "best plumber in Minneapolis" five times.

## Does llms.txt Actually Work?

The llms.txt convention is still relatively new, but the evidence is promising:

- AI models do crawl and index llms.txt files when they're available
- Businesses with llms.txt provide clearer signals for AI recommendation systems
- The structured format reduces the chance of AI misinterpreting your business
- Early adopters report appearing more frequently in AI-generated recommendations

The downside risk is essentially zero — it's a free text file that takes minutes to create. The upside is getting discovered by the fastest-growing search channel in history.

## Common Questions About llms.txt

**Will this replace my website?**
No. Your website is still your primary online presence. llms.txt supplements it by giving AI a quick, structured summary. Think of it as a cheat sheet, not a replacement.

**Is this the same as robots.txt?**
Different purpose. robots.txt tells search crawlers which pages to index. llms.txt tells AI models what your business is about. You should have both.

**Do I need technical skills?**
If you can write a text document and upload a file to your website, you can create an llms.txt. That's all it takes.

**How often should I update it?**
Whenever something meaningful changes — new service, new location, new hours, updated phone number. A quarterly review is a good habit.

**Can I have both llms.txt and structured schema markup?**
Absolutely — and you should. They serve complementary purposes. Schema markup helps Google and AI understand specific pages on your site. llms.txt gives a business-level overview.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What file format should llms.txt be in?**
Plain text (UTF-8). Use Markdown-style headings with `#` for structure. No HTML, no special formatting needed.

**Where exactly do I put the llms.txt file?**
At the root of your domain, so it's accessible at `yourwebsite.com/llms.txt`. Same level as your homepage.

**Do all AI assistants use llms.txt?**
Not all, but the convention is gaining adoption. Even for AI systems that don't specifically look for llms.txt, having well-structured information about your business at a known URL improves discoverability.

**Can llms.txt hurt my SEO?**
No. It's a plain text file that search engines will either ignore or treat as a low-priority page. It has no negative impact on your existing SEO.

**How long should my llms.txt file be?**
There's no strict limit, but aim for completeness without padding. Most local business llms.txt files are 50-150 lines. Include everything relevant, skip the fluff.
