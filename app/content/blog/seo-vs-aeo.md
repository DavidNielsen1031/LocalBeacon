If you run a local business, you've probably been told that SEO is important. And it is — Google Search is still how most people find local services.

But there's a new acronym on the block: **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)**. And it's not just another marketing buzzword. It represents a fundamental shift in how customers discover businesses.

So what's the difference? Do you need both? And how do you handle it all without hiring a full marketing team?

Let's break it down.

## SEO: The Foundation You Already Know

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is about making your business appear in Google Search results. When someone searches "plumber near me" or "best dentist in Eagan," SEO determines which businesses show up — and in what order.

For local businesses, SEO comes down to a few core things:

- **Google Business Profile optimization** — complete profile, regular posts, review responses
- **Website content** — service pages, location pages, blog posts
- **Technical SEO** — fast loading times, mobile-friendly design, proper meta tags
- **Citations and backlinks** — consistent NAP (name, address, phone) across directories
- **Reviews** — volume, recency, and rating

SEO has been the dominant local marketing strategy for 15+ years. It works. Businesses that do it well get calls. Businesses that don't get overlooked.

## AEO: The New Layer

AEO is about making your business visible to **AI-powered answer engines** — ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Claude, Siri, and Alexa.

These tools don't show a list of 10 results. They give **one answer**. When someone asks "Who's a reliable HVAC company in Apple Valley?", the AI picks a business to recommend. If it doesn't pick you, you don't get a consolation prize on page two — you get nothing.

AEO focuses on:

- **Structured data and schema markup** — helping AI parse your business information
- **FAQ content** — answering the questions customers actually ask
- **llms.txt** — a dedicated file that tells AI about your business
- **Entity clarity** — making sure AI knows exactly what your business is, does, and where it operates
- **Content freshness** — recent posts and updates signal an active, reliable business

## The Key Differences

| | SEO | AEO |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | Rank in a list of results | Be the recommended answer |
| **Platform** | Google Search, Bing | ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI, Siri |
| **Format** | Blue links, map pack | Conversational text, direct citation |
| **Ranking signals** | Backlinks, keywords, authority | Structured data, clarity, recency |
| **User behavior** | Click through results, compare | Accept the AI's recommendation |
| **Competition** | Top 3-10 get traffic | Top 1 gets the mention |
| **Feedback loop** | Search Console data, rankings | Harder to track, newer tools emerging |

## Where SEO and AEO Overlap

Here's the good news: a lot of what's good for SEO is also good for AEO. You're not starting from scratch.

**Google Business Profile** — essential for both. AI engines pull heavily from GBP data when making local recommendations.

**Reviews** — Google uses reviews for rankings. AI uses reviews for recommendations. Either way, more positive, recent reviews help you.

**Website content** — well-written service pages help you rank in Google and get cited by AI. The quality of the content matters for both.

**Mobile-friendly, fast website** — Google penalizes slow sites. AI crawlers also prefer sites that are easy to parse.

**Local citations** — consistent business information across directories helps Google trust your data, and helps AI confirm your details.

The things that overlap — GBP, reviews, quality content, technical basics — should be your priority because they pay dividends in both channels.

## Where They Diverge

**Schema markup** matters more for AEO. Google uses it, but AI models rely on it much more heavily to understand your business programmatically.

**llms.txt** is AEO-specific. Google doesn't use llms.txt (it has its own crawling methods). But AI assistants are increasingly looking for this file as a structured business summary.

**FAQ content** has always been good for SEO, but it's critical for AEO. AI models are trained to match questions with answers, and well-structured FAQs are exactly the format they look for.

**Content freshness** matters more for AEO than many realize. AI models weight recency heavily — a business that posted to Google last week looks more reliable than one that hasn't posted in 6 months.

**Keyword density** matters less for AEO. AI models understand context and meaning, so writing naturally about your services is more effective than optimizing for specific keyword phrases.

## How to Invest in Both Without Losing Your Mind

If you're a busy business owner (and who isn't?), the thought of managing both SEO and AEO can feel overwhelming. Here's a practical approach:

### Level 1: The Basics (Do This Now)

1. Make sure your Google Business Profile is 100% complete
2. Respond to every review (good and bad)
3. Post to your GBP weekly
4. Create or update an llms.txt file
5. Add FAQ schema markup to your website

Time investment: 2-3 hours one-time, then 30 minutes per week.

### Level 2: Building Out (Next 30 Days)

1. Create a service page for each service you offer
2. Create a location page for each city you serve
3. Add LocalBusiness schema markup to your site
4. Write 5-10 FAQ answers about your most common customer questions
5. Make sure your NAP info is consistent across all directories

Time investment: 5-10 hours over a month.

### Level 3: Competitive Advantage (Ongoing)

1. Publish regular blog content about your industry and local area
2. Monitor AI visibility (ask AI assistants about your services quarterly)
3. Update llms.txt and schema markup as your business evolves
4. Track which review responses lead to follow-up engagement
5. Test AI recommendations after each optimization round

Time investment: 2-4 hours per month.

## The Bottom Line

SEO and AEO aren't competing strategies — they're complementary layers of your online visibility. SEO keeps you visible in Google Search, where most local discovery still happens. AEO makes sure you're the business AI assistants recommend as conversational search grows.

The businesses that do both will have a significant advantage over those doing neither — or only one. And since many of the fundamentals overlap, it's not twice the work. It's about adding a few AEO-specific optimizations (structured data, llms.txt, FAQ content) on top of the SEO work you're probably already doing.

Start with what overlaps. Add AEO-specific elements. Stay consistent. That's the playbook.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I just focus on SEO and ignore AEO?**
You can, but you'd be leaving a growing channel unaddressed. AI-driven search is increasing every month. Starting AEO now gives you a head start before it becomes as competitive as SEO.

**Is AEO more important than SEO?**
Not yet, and probably not for a while. Google Search still drives more local business discovery than AI assistants. But the gap is closing, and AEO is worth investing in now.

**How do I know if AEO is working?**
Ask AI assistants about your services in your area and see if you get recommended. Tools like LocalBeacon also provide an AI Readiness Score that tracks your visibility to AI search engines.

**Does my website builder affect AEO?**
Somewhat. Website builders that make it easy to add structured data (schema markup) and custom files (llms.txt) are better for AEO. Most modern builders support this.

**How much should I spend on AEO vs SEO?**
Most local businesses should spend about 70% of their effort on overlapping activities (GBP, reviews, content, citations) and 30% on AEO-specific items (schema, llms.txt, FAQ markup). The overlap work benefits both.
