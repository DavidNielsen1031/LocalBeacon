# 7 Things Roofing Companies Should Do for AI Visibility

Spring is the roofing industry's Super Bowl. Hail storms roll through, gutters are clogged, and suddenly every homeowner is inspecting their shingles with fresh anxiety. But here's what's changed in the last two years: a growing chunk of those homeowners aren't opening Google anymore. They're typing into ChatGPT, Perplexity, or asking Siri, "find me a reputable roofer near me who does insurance claims."

If AI doesn't know who you are, you don't get that call.

This isn't a problem with traditional SEO. You can rank #1 on Google and still be completely absent from AI-generated recommendations. That's because AI answer engines pull from a different set of signals — and roofing companies are, frankly, behind on all of them.

Here are 7 things to fix before storm season hits.

---

## 1. Build a Dedicated Services Page for Every Roof Type You Work On

Most roofing websites have a single "Services" page that lists everything in a bullet list. That's fine for humans skimming. It's terrible for AI systems trying to answer "who does metal roofing in [city]?"

AI models are trained to match specific queries to specific content. If you want to show up when someone asks about metal roofing, you need a page about metal roofing — with complete information about the service, the materials you use, the typical timeline, cost ranges, and why a homeowner would choose it.

Do this for every major service:
- Asphalt shingle replacement
- Metal roofing installation
- Storm damage repair
- Flat/commercial roofing
- Roof inspections
- Insurance claim assistance

Each page should answer the questions a homeowner would ask an AI. Not just "we do this" — but *what it involves, how long it takes, what it costs, and what to watch out for*.

---

## 2. Own Your Insurance Claim Process in Writing

"Who does insurance claims for storm damage?" is one of the most common roofing queries AI sees after a bad weather event. If your site doesn't clearly explain your insurance claim workflow, you're not getting recommended.

Write out the process step by step. What happens when a homeowner calls after storm damage? Do you offer free inspections? How do you work with adjusters? What documentation do you help prepare?

This information serves double duty: it's genuinely useful content that builds trust with homeowners, *and* it's exactly the kind of structured, informative text that AI systems draw on when answering insurance-related roofing questions.

---

## 3. Get Your Google Business Profile to 100% — Including the Q&A Section

Your Google Business Profile is one of the primary sources AI tools use to understand local businesses. It's not just for Google anymore.

Fill every field:
- Business description (use natural language, include your service area and key services)
- All service categories selected accurately
- Current hours with holiday exceptions
- Photos — real job photos, not stock images
- FAQ section with the questions homeowners actually ask

The Q&A section is especially underused by roofing companies. Add 8-10 real questions: "Do you offer free roof inspections?" "Do you work with insurance companies?" "How long does a full roof replacement take?" Answer them completely. AI models treat this as authoritative first-party information.

---

## 4. Schema Markup Your Service Pages

Schema markup is structured data you add to your website that explicitly tells search engines and AI systems what your content means. It's the difference between AI guessing you're a roofing company and *knowing* you're a roofing company that serves homeowners in three specific counties.

For a roofing business, the most important schema types are:
- **LocalBusiness** (with `@type: "RoofingContractor"`) — your core identity
- **Service** — one per service page, with name, description, and `areaServed`
- **Review / AggregateRating** — your review score surfaced in structured form
- **FAQPage** — on any page with questions and answers

If you're on WordPress, a plugin like RankMath or Yoast handles most of this. On custom sites, you'll need a developer to add the JSON-LD blocks. It's a one-time investment that pays dividends every time an AI processes your site.

---

## 5. Respond to Every Review — Especially the Negative Ones

AI systems that recommend local businesses weight reviews heavily — not just the star rating, but the *patterns* they see in review content and responses.

A roofing company with 4.6 stars and 80 thoughtful responses looks very different to an AI than one with 4.6 stars and 12 responses. The active engagement signals a legitimate, attentive business.

For roofing specifically:
- Respond to every review within 48 hours
- In positive responses, naturally mention the type of work done ("Glad the asphalt replacement came out great…")
- In negative responses, be calm and solution-focused — AI models treat your response as evidence of how you handle problems
- Never use the same template twice — unique responses signal authenticity

Reviews that mention specific services, locations, or materials also help AI understand what you do and where you do it. Encourage satisfied customers to be specific: "They replaced our entire asphalt roof after the hail storm last May" is 10x more valuable than "Great job!"

---

## 6. Create a Storm Damage FAQ Page (and Update It Seasonally)

When storm season hits, AI chatbots field thousands of queries like "what should I do after hail damage to my roof?" and "how do I know if I need a full replacement or just repairs?"

If your site has a comprehensive, honest, genuinely helpful FAQ that answers these questions — and it's linked prominently from your homepage — you become a source AI systems learn to cite.

A strong storm damage FAQ covers:
- How to document damage for an insurance claim (photos, dates, what adjusters look for)
- The difference between minor granule loss and structural damage
- Timeline from inspection to completed repair
- What "storm chaser" contractors are and how to avoid them
- Whether your homeowner's insurance covers different types of roof damage

Update this page each spring with current information. Date-stamped, recently updated content signals to AI that your information is fresh and reliable.

---

## 7. Add an llms.txt File to Your Website

This one is new but increasingly important. An `llms.txt` file is a simple text file you place at `yourroofingcompany.com/llms.txt` that tells AI crawlers exactly what your business does, where you operate, and what pages are most important for understanding your services.

Think of it as a cover letter for AI systems — a human-readable summary that helps them quickly understand who you are without having to parse your entire website.

A basic `llms.txt` for a roofing company looks like this:

```
# [Your Company Name] — Roofing Contractor

We are a licensed roofing contractor serving [City, State] and surrounding areas.

## Services
- Asphalt shingle roof replacement
- Metal roofing installation
- Storm damage repair and insurance claim assistance
- Roof inspections
- Commercial flat roofing

## Service Area
[List your counties/cities]

## Key Pages
- /services/storm-damage-repair
- /services/roof-replacement
- /insurance-claims
- /about
- /contact
```

It takes 20 minutes to create and costs nothing. Very few roofing companies have one, which means adding it immediately differentiates you with AI crawlers that support it — and the list is growing.

## The Bottom Line

Roofing is a high-urgency, high-trust category. When a homeowner needs a roofer, they need one *now* and they need to trust that this person won't disappear after taking their insurance money. AI systems know this, and they tend to recommend businesses that look established, transparent, and responsive.

The 7 steps above build exactly that profile. None of them are technically complex. Most are content and information you should have on your site anyway. The difference is structuring it in a way that AI systems can find, parse, and confidently recommend to homeowners in your area.

Storm season doesn't wait. Neither should you.
