# AI Citation Patterns — What Gets Cited by Each Engine

*Last updated: March 2, 2026*
*Sources: r/seogrowth 1,400+ citation analysis, HubSpot AEO report, Search Engine Land*

---

## Universal Patterns (All Engines)

1. **FAQ format dramatically increases citations** — Q&A pairs are the single most extractable content type
2. **First clear answer wins** — AI extracts the first relevant passage, not the best one buried on page 3
3. **2-7 domains cited per response** — You're competing for a tiny number of slots
4. **Earned media > owned content** — Third-party coverage (reviews, articles, Reddit mentions) outweighs your own website for trust
5. **Entity consistency builds trust** — Same NAP everywhere, proper sameAs linking, knowledge panel
6. **Freshness matters** — Content with 2025/2026 dates cited over identical undated content
7. **"Authoritative consensus"** — You don't need to be #1 on Google, you need to be part of the consensus (cited by multiple sources)

## ChatGPT-Specific

- **Conversational content preferred** — Writes that read like a human explaining something
- **Comprehensive topic coverage** — Deeper pages beat thinner ones
- **Wikipedia-style factual density** — Stats, numbers, specifics get cited
- **FAQ schema heavily weighted** — Having FAQPage schema nearly guarantees consideration
- **Memory/training data + real-time search hybrid** — Both your historic web presence and current content matter
- **Local queries increasingly use browsing** — ChatGPT's browse mode checks live sites for local recommendations

## Perplexity-Specific

- **Strong citation practices required** — Perplexity shows source URLs prominently
- **Factual accuracy verification** — Cross-references multiple sources
- **Recent content strongly favored** — Perplexity indexes more frequently
- **Well-sourced content** — Pages that cite their own sources get cited more
- **Reddit and forum content heavily weighted** — Community discussions are a major source

## Google AI Overviews-Specific

- **Traditional SEO signals still matter** — AI Overviews pull from Google's search index
- **Schema markup heavily weighted** — Structured data helps Google parse content
- **Local Business schema + reviews** — Strong signal for local queries
- **16% of desktop searches now show AI Overviews** — Growing rapidly
- **Conversion tracking possible** — Track via GA4 with AI Overview attribution

## Claude-Specific

- **Structured, well-organized content** — Clean heading hierarchy matters
- **llms.txt helps significantly** — Claude actively checks for this file
- **Authoritative, well-written sources** — Quality of prose matters more than keyword density
- **Tends to cite fewer sources but with higher confidence**

## Local Business Citation Strategies

### What query patterns trigger local AI recommendations?

1. "Best [service] in [city]" — Most common, highest intent
2. "Who is the most trusted [service] near me" — Trust signals critical
3. "[Service] cost in [city]" — Price-focused, FAQ content wins
4. "Emergency [service] [city]" — Urgency queries, 24/7 availability matters
5. "[Service] reviews [city]" — Review aggregation and schema crucial

### What content gets cited for local queries?

1. **Google Business Profile data** — Still the primary source for many AI engines
2. **Yelp and review aggregator pages** — Heavy weight for local recommendations
3. **FAQ pages with local pricing** — "How much does X cost in [city]?"
4. **City-specific service pages** — Dedicated pages per service area
5. **Blog posts answering local questions** — "Best time to replace your furnace in Minnesota"
6. **llms.txt with service area data** — Direct signal to AI crawlers

## Competitive Analysis Template

When checking a competitor's AI presence:

1. Ask ChatGPT: "Who is the best [category] in [city]?"
2. Ask Perplexity: same query
3. Note: which businesses are mentioned? What sources are cited?
4. Check those businesses for: llms.txt, schema markup, FAQ content, review count
5. Identify gaps: what do they have that the client doesn't?
